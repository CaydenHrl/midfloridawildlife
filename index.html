Before you call

Questions & Answers

Straight answers to what most folks ask before booking an inspection.

Is it safe to remove the animals myself? +

We don't recommend it. Wild animals can bite, scratch, or carry disease, and DIY traps often miss the real entry points — so the problem just comes back. A licensed specialist removes the animal safely and identifies every way it got in, not just the obvious one.

Are bats or raccoons protected by law in Florida? +

Yes — bats are protected under both Florida and federal law, and there are strict rules around when and how exclusion can be performed. Raccoons and other native wildlife also fall under state wildlife regulations. We're licensed to handle removal and exclusion legally and humanely, so you don't have to navigate that yourself.

How long does the exclusion process take? +

It depends on the animal and the extent of the problem. Some jobs — like sealing a single squirrel entry point — can be done in one visit. Others, like a larger raccoon or bat situation, may take a few follow-up visits over one to several weeks to make sure every animal is out and every entry point is sealed for good.

Do you guarantee your work? +

Yes. Our exclusion repairs come with a 1-year warranty against future animal intrusion through the same entry points, with extended warranty options available. The only things our warranty doesn't cover are humans and mother nature.

What happens to the animals after they're removed? +

We work with local wildlife rehabilitation groups whenever possible, so animals are cared for and released back into their natural habitat rather than simply disposed of. We take an eco-friendly, humane approach throughout.

What areas do you serve? +

We're based in the 352 area and regularly serve Marion, Sumter, Lake, and Citrus counties. If you're outside that area, give us a call anyway — if the job's worth the drive, we'll be there.

How much does an inspection cost? +

Home inspections are free. We'll check for entry points, signs of activity, and conducive conditions, then walk you through exactly what we find and what it'll take to fix it — no obligation.
