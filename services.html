Our Services

Five intruders, one solution: identify the entry, remove the animal, seal it shut.

Rodents

Rat & Mouse Removal

Rats in a home pose serious health risks — diseases like hantavirus and salmonellosis spread through droppings and urine. Left alone, they cause real damage and real risk:

  • Chewed wiring that can spark electrical fires
  • Damaged insulation and walls
  • Contaminated food and water sources
  • Triggered or worsened allergies and asthma
  • An open door for ticks, fleas, and other pests

We remove the rodents, sanitize the affected areas, and use proven exclusion methods to keep them from coming back.

Protected species

Bat Removal & Exclusion

Bats are social animals — if you've spotted one, a colony is usually already moving in. They're a vital part of Florida's ecosystem, but they're unsanitary, risky houseguests:

  • Droppings (guano) can cause serious respiratory illness
  • Bats are a known carrier of rabies
  • Colonies grow fast if left unaddressed
State-protected: Bats are protected under both Florida and federal law. Exclusion must be performed by a licensed wildlife specialist, on the correct legal timeline — we know exactly how.

A free inspection identifies every entry point so we can build a safe, legal exclusion plan for your home.

Squirrel Removal

Squirrels chew through roof vents to get inside, and once they're in, the damage adds up fast:

  • Chewed electrical wires — a real fire hazard
  • Shredded insulation, hurting your home's energy efficiency
  • Torn-up soffits, shingles, and wood structure

We handle safe, effective removal and seal the entry points so the next squirrel doesn't try the same trick.

Nuisance wildlife

Opossum Removal

Opossums are genuinely good for the ecosystem — natural pest controllers. But once they're raiding the garbage, eating pet food, or denning in your attic or crawl space, they become a real problem:

  • Flea- and tick-borne disease risk
  • Unsanitary conditions from feces and urine
  • Property damage from chewing and scratching
  • Lingering odors from droppings
  • Attracting other pests to the same space

We remove them humanely and close off the access points for good.

Raccoon Removal

Don't let the masked-bandit look fool you — raccoons in the attic are a genuine hazard:

  • Disease risk, including rabies and raccoon roundworm
  • Gnawed wiring that creates real fire hazards
  • Roof damage that invites mold
  • Health risks from urine and feces exposure

Contact a Mid-Florida specialist for safe, professional removal — especially if you suspect babies are involved.
